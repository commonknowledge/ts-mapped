Any boundary visible on the map — a constituency, a ward, a local authority — can be saved as a custom area with one click. Custom areas can then be used to filter your marker data sources to show only the records that fall within them.

## Adding a boundary to your areas list

### 1. Load a boundary set

Expand the **Data Visualisation** layer in the left panel and select a boundary type (e.g. Westminster Parliamentary Constituencies). The boundaries will appear on the map.

### 2. Click a boundary

Click any boundary on the map. The Inspector will open showing that boundary's details.

### 3. Click "Add to areas"

Click the **Add to areas** button in the Inspector. The boundary's geometry is saved to your **Areas** layer under the boundary's name.

You'll see it appear immediately in the Areas list in the left panel.

## Using the area to filter markers

Once a boundary has been added to your areas list, your Markers layers will automatically filter to show only records within your defined areas. If you've added multiple areas, records within any of them will be shown.

This is useful when you want to focus on a specific constituency or ward without having to draw a shape manually — the boundary geometry is already precise.

## Removing an area

To remove a custom area, open the **Areas** layer in the left panel, click the area, and select **Delete** from the Inspector.
