The Data Visualisation panel is where you control how boundary maps (choropleths) look — colour schemes, scale types, opacity, and layout. It sits on the right side of the map editor and works alongside the Legend on the left.

## Opening the panel

### 1. Expand the Data Visualisation layer

In the left panel of the map editor, find the **Data Visualisation** section and click to expand it.

### 2. Click the paintbrush icon

Click the paintbrush icon next to the Data Visualisation layer. The panel will open on the right side of the screen.

The Style section inside the panel only becomes active once you've selected a boundary type, data source, and column in the Legend. If the panel looks greyed out, set those up first.

## What the panel contains

### Layout

Switch between two layout modes:

- **Geographic** — boundaries are drawn in their true geographical positions and shapes (default).
- **Hex Map** — boundaries are represented as equal-sized hexagons arranged in a rough geographic layout. Useful when smaller areas like London would otherwise be hard to see.

### Style

- **Colour scale type** — choose how values map to colours: [Gradient](/docs/colour-a-boundary-by-data), [Stepped](/docs/stepped-colour-scale), or Categorical.
- **Colour scheme** — pick a palette: Sequential, Red-Blue, Green-Yellow-Red, Viridis, Plasma, Diverging, or Custom.
- **Reverse** — flip the colour direction (e.g. make high values dark instead of light).
- **Opacity** — slide to adjust the transparency of the boundary fill (0–100%).

The Style section is hidden when a secondary column is selected for [bivariate visualisation](/docs/bivariate-visualisation), since the bivariate colour matrix takes over.
