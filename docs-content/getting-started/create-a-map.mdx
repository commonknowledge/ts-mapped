Mapped lets you build interactive, embeddable maps from your existing data in just a few steps. Once you've created a map, you can add data source layers, customise the styling, and share it publicly or keep it private within your organisation.

## Creating your first map

### 1. Open the Maps dashboard

Navigate to the **Maps** section from the main navigation. You'll see a list of all maps belonging to your organisation.

![Maps dashboard](/docs/create-a-map/dashboard.png)

### 2. Click "New Map"

Select **New Map** in the top-right corner. A dialogue will appear asking you to name your map.

### 3. Name your map

Enter a descriptive name — you can change this later. Click **Create** to proceed.

### 4. Configure your map

You'll be taken to the map editor. From here you can:

- **Add layers** — connect a data source and choose a visualisation type (points, choropleth, etc.)
- **Set the base map style** — choose from light, dark, satellite, or street styles
- **Adjust the default viewport** — pan and zoom to the area you want users to see first

### 5. Save and publish

Click **Save** at any time to preserve your changes. When you're ready to share the map, head to **Publish** to make it publicly accessible or generate an embed code.
